Fishes and Dolphins and Sharks

India’s notebook
29 June 2015

India: That’s a spotty dolphin. That’s a killer whale… hammerhead shark… great white shark, and nurse shark, and that’s an eel. That’s one of those tiger fishies — but I forgot the long scraggly things.

Taj: They’re tiger sharks. Tiger sharks.

India: Yeah tiger sharks. I mean lion sharks —

समाधिBot: Lionfish.

India: Yeah lionfish. There’s a little… minnow… minnow — yeah minnow. This is a micro-minnow —

Taj: India, did you draw… a box shark?

India: There’s no such thing as a box shark.

समाधिBot: What’s this funny looking one?

India: Um, it’s this kind of large lumpy thing that lurks in the deep. I forgot to draw the ginormous teeth on it, because it has ginormous teeth. And I forgot to draw the lure thing.

Taj: You forgot to draw the shark whale also.

India: I couldn’t fit it on there.

Taj: Because shark whales are —

India: Anywayyy… this is a piranha — this is called a red bellied piranha. That’s ummm… that’s a catfish — yeah a catfish, and that’s a butterfly fish. Annnd, is that all of them?
